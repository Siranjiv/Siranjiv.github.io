Ray Tracing in C/C++

This project was built in C/C++ with CPU-based backward ray tracing from a fixed camera at (0,0,0). I implemented all core features and completed extra credit for recursive reflection, soft shadows, anti-aliasing, and animation output.

Core Requirements Implemented

What I built

  • Uniform camera rays with 60 degree FOV at 640x480 final output resolution.
  • Ray-object intersection for both triangles and spheres.
  • Phong illumination with diffuse/specular shading and clamped dot products.
  • Per-light shadow rays to determine occlusion and direct lighting contribution.
  • Barycentric interpolation on triangle hits for normals, material coefficients, and shininess.
  • Global ambient term added once per hit and final color channel clamping to [0,1].
  • Still image output support (JPEG) in addition to on-screen rendering.
Extra Credit Completed

Advanced features

  • Recursive reflection using weighted local + reflected contribution.
  • Soft shadows with area-light style sampling.
  • Anti-aliasing with multi-sample style supersampling.
  • Animation frame rendering exported as GIF sequence.

These results were tested across assignment scenes (including toy, table, spheres, and SIGGRAPH) to validate shading quality, reflections, and temporal consistency in animation.
